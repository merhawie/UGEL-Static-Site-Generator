article 6: Customer Identification and Due Diligence

<ul>
			<li>1 - : Financial institutions may not keep anonymous accounts or accounts in fictitious.<ul>
			</ul></li>			<li>2 - : Financial institutions may not enter mto, or continue, correspondent banking relationships with shell banks.<ul>
			</ul></li>			<li>3 - : Financial institutions shall undertake customer due diligence measures when:<ul>
						<li>a - : establishing business relations with a customer;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>b - : carrying out occasional cash transaction with a customer exceeding USD 10,000 or its equivalent in other currencies, and shall include situations where the transaction is carried out in a single operation or in several operations that appear to be linked or structured;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>c - : there is a suspicion of money laundering or terrorist financing, regardless of any exemptions or thresholds provided under this Proclamation; and<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>d - : they have doubts about the veracity or adequacy of previously-obtained customer identification data.<ul>
						</ul></li>			</ul></li>			<li>4 - : Financial institutions shall identify the customer, whether regular or occasional, natural or juridical person or legal arrangement, and verify that customer’s identity using, as much as possible, reliable independent source documents, data or information.<ul>
			</ul></li>			<li>5 - : Identification requirements for natural persons shall include:<ul>
						<li>a - : given or legal name and all other names used;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>b - : identity card or residence permit or passport;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>c - : permanent address;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>d - : telephone number, fax number, mailing and e-mail address, if available;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>e - : date and place of birth;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>f - : nationality;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>g - : occupation, public position held and&#x2F;or name of employer, if any;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>h - : type of account; and<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>i - : signed statement certifying accuracy of the information provided.<ul>
						</ul></li>			</ul></li>			<li>6 - : For customers that are juridical persons or legal arrangements, financial institutions shall;<ul>
						<li>a - : take reasonable measures to understand the ownership and control structure of the customer and determine who the natural persons that ultimately own or control the juridical person or arrangement arc. including those natural persons who exercise ultimate effective control over the juridical person or arrangement;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>b - : verify that any person purporting to act on behalf of the customer is so authorized, and identify and verify the identity of that person;<ul>
						</ul></li>						<li>c - : verify the legal status of the juridical person or legal arrangement at a minimum by obtaining proof of incorporation or similar evidence of establishment or existence. Information concerning the juridical person or legal arrangement’s shall include:<ul>
									<li>i - : name;<ul>
									</ul></li>									<li>ii - : legal form;<ul>
									</ul></li>									<li>iii - : some form of official identification number such as tax identification number, if available;<ul>
									</ul></li>									<li>iv - : address which includes country, region&#x2F;city&#x2F;town&#x2F;zonal administration in which the head office is located and, if available, house number, mailing address, telephone number and fax number;<ul>
									</ul></li>									<li>v - : names of the general manager or chief executive officer and of directors, if applicable;<ul>
									</ul></li>									<li>vi - : provisions regulating the power to bind the juridical person or arrangement;<ul>
									</ul></li>									<li>vii - : the resolution of the board of directors, if applicable, or any other authorized body or person that authorizes to open an account; and<ul>
									</ul></li>									<li>viii - : identification of those who have authority to operate the accounts.<ul>
									</ul></li>						</ul></li>			</ul></li>			<li>7 - : In carrying out transactions with any person, a financial institution shall identify the ultimate beneficial owner and take reasonable measures to verify the identity of the beneficial owner using relevant information or data obtained from a reliable source such that the financial institution is satisfied that it knows who the beneficial owner is. A financial institution shall, for all its customers, determine whether the customer is acting on his own behalf or on behalf of another person and, if the customer is found to be another person, it shall take reasonable steps to obtain sufficient identification data to verify the identity of that other person.<ul>
			</ul></li>			<li>8 - : Establishment of a financial institution&#39;s new business relationship with a politically- exposed person shall be approved by a senior management member of the financial institution.<ul>
			</ul></li>			<li>9 - : Where a customer has been accepted and the customer or beneficial owner is subsequently found to be, or subsequently becomes, a politically-exposed person, continuation of business relationship with such person shall be approved by a senior mannoment member of the financial institution.<ul>
			</ul></li>			<li>10 - : Financial institutions shall take reasonable measures to establish the source of wealth and the source of funds of customers and beneficial owners identified as politically- exposed persons.<ul>
			</ul></li>			<li>11 - : Financial institutions shall obtain information on the purpose and intended nature of the business relationship.<ul>
			</ul></li>			<li>12 - : Banks shall perform enhanced due diligence on high risk categories of customers, business relationships or transactions.<ul>
			</ul></li>			<li>13 - : Financial institutions shall give particular attention to business relationships and transactions with natural and judicial persons from countries which do not or insufficiently apply anti-money laundering and combating terrorist financing laws.<ul>
			</ul></li></ul>
